Who is Using AI?

Looking at AI tools used by education level, daily AI usage by age, and a geographic distribution of students by education level, we were able to paint a picture of the demographic of students using AI in Pakistan. Education level is categorized into three groups: 'School', 'College', and 'University.' In Pakistan, 'School' covers primary and secondary education (Grades 1-10), 'College' handles grades 11-12 and sometimes 2-year degrees, and 'University' consists of undergraduate or graduate degrees. The following stacked bar chart shows the distribution of education level for each AI tool.

Overall, Gemini was the most used AI application among respondents, with 24 total students reporting it as their go-to tool. Notion AI was reported the least, with only 12 respondents claiming it as their most used tool. Notion AI is an organizing, editing, and structuring tool used to create documents and manage databases directly in your workspace. Gemini on the other hand is a more powerful large language model that acts as an independent assistant to help with research and generate ideas. Gemini is generally a more versatile tool, explaining why it might be more popular among students. Among all AI tools used, students at the 'School' level used AI tools more or as much as all other levels of education for each tool. This is likely a reflection of the population of respondents in the data. Students at the 'College' level reported using Gemini as their main tool and both students at the 'University' level and students at the 'School' level reported using Grammarly as their main AI tool. Grammarly is a communication assistant that helps to impove spelling, grammar, tone and provides other writing suggestions. This is an interesting insight into the similarities of usage of AI among students in the early stages of their educational career and those further into their careers. Students at the 'School' level tended to use Grammary, Gemini, ChatGPT, and Copilot at relatively equal levels, whereas students in 'College' had much more variability in their responses.

The correlation between daily AI usage in hours and student age was very weak. For 'College' students and 'School' students, the correlation was slightly negative, but only yielded p-values greater than 0.05, indicating a lack of statistical significance. At the 'University' level, the relationship was even weaker, yielding a slightly positive correlation with a p-value of 0.89. The scatterplot indicates a clear lack of relationship between usage hours and age for this dataset. It's possible that more data would allow us to see a clearer picture for the relationship.

AI usage in Pakistan was distributed across four major cities in Pakistan: Islamabad, Faisalabad, Lahore, and Multan. 'University' students were present in all cities but tended to be concentrated in Lahore. Students at the 'School' level tended to be concentrated all around Multan. 'College' student respondents were also present in every city. The map highlights that AI usage is prevalent across cities, regardless of education level.
